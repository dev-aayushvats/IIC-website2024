More About the Event

Rules

  • The team size should be 2 members
  • Usage of laptops, mobile phones, or any smart accessories will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • All the event-specific rules and guidelines will be conveyed before the event
  • Organizers reserve the right to change, modify or postpone any aspect of the event without prior notice. In case of any dispute, the decision of the organizers shall be final and binding
  • Any other form of cheating or plagiarism will also result in immediate disqualification.

Rounds

  • The event will take place in two rounds.
  • Participants should register for the event through the registration form. The link for submissions will be shared via email. Based on the performance, teams will be shortlisted for the final round.

    • Round 1 : MCQ
    • Each team receives a question sheet with 15 MCQs.
    • Topics include personal finance, investments, financial markets, banking, economics, and more.
    • Each question has four options: A, B, C, and D.
    • Teams should write or circle their responses (A, B, C, or D) on the question sheet.
    • All teams must submit their answer sheets within 5 minutes. The end of the timeframe will be signaled by a buzzer.
    • Ensure completion within the designated time. Late submissions won't be accepted.
    • Correct Answer: +4 points || Incorrect Answer: -2 points || Unanswered: 0 points
    • Once the time is up, teams must promptly submit their answer sheets to the quiz organizers. The quiz organizers will evaluate the answer sheets and calculate the scores. The quizmaster's decision on all scoring matters is final.
    • Tiebreaker: In the event of a tie between two or more teams, tiebreaker questions may be used to determine the final winner
    • The top 5 teams from Round 1 will qualify for the final round i.e. round 2.

    Round 2 : Final oral Q&A round
    • Each team will receive two questions.
    • The quizmaster will pose a question to a specific team, and they will have 30 seconds to respond. Our vigilant quizmaster will announce when time's up.
    • A correct answer earns the team 4 points, showcasing their financial prowess. If a team is stumped, they have the option to pass the question to the next team in line, adding a strategic twist to the challenge.
    • If a team is unable or decides not to answer, they can pass the question to the next team in line. The team passing the question won't be penalized, but they will miss the chance to score on that question.
    • The team receiving the passed question will have the opportunity to answer and earn +2 points for a correct response. The question will keep circulating from team to team until it reaches the final team, ensuring everyone gets their shot at scoring.
    • Correct Answer (on the first attempt): +4 points || Correct Answer (after being passed): +2 points || Incorrect Answer or No Answer: 0 points
    • Tiebreaker: Tiebreaker questions determine the winner in case of a tie. The organizers will decide the nature and number of tiebreaker questions.

    Final Scores and Winner
    • The overall winner will be determined by combining the final scores from Round 1 and Round 2.
    • The team with the highest total score will be declared the Finance For All Quiz winner with the first and second runner-up following suit.

F.A.Q.

  • How many members can a team have?

    A team must consist 4 members.

  • Will the points from round 1 be considered for the declaration of the winner?

    Yes, the winner will be determined based on the cumulative score of both Round 1 and Round 2.

  • Do all teams participate in both the Rounds?

    No, only the top 5 teams from Round 1 qualify for Round 2.

  • Is there negative marking in both the rounds?

    In Round 1, a correct answer fetches 4 points while an incorrect answer fetches -2 points. There is no negative marking in Round 2.
